The applicant's legal classification determines what information will be required on subsequent screens. For example, an applicant with a legal classification of "Individual" will be required to enter information that is different from the information that an applicant with a legal classification of "Corporation" or "Limited Liability Company" must enter.

You must select one classification type for the applicant from the list of legal classification choices on this page. If you select "Other" for the applicant's legal classification, a text box will open in which you must describe the applicant's legal classification. If you do not choose a legal classification, you will receive an error message and will be unable to continue to the next page o 6or the Applicant Name screen depend on which legal classification you specified on the Applicant Legal Classification screen. Consortium

The data entry fields for the Applicant Name screen for Applicant Name depend on which legal classification you specified on the Applicant Legal Classification screen. In this case, you are filing as a Consortium, so the system is asking for Entity Name and Jurisdiction of Formation. For a Consortium, Jurisdiction of Formation refers to the state under whose laws the Consortium was formed/established.

The data entry fields for the Applicant Name screen for Applicant Name depend on which legal classification you specified on the Applicant Legal Classification screen.
